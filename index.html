About Me

I’m a PhD student in electronic engineering and radioglaciology based at University College London, using high-frequency phase-sensitive radar to produce synthetic aperture radar (SAR) imagery of ice sheets and glaciers. More broadly, I’m interested by applications of remote monitoring and ground-based electronic sensing to glaciology and glacial hydrology. In my spare time, I enjoy mountaineering, cycling and painting.

Hopefully there's something on here to take your interest, and please don't hesitate to get in touch should you have any questions.
